Different Ways to Game

From phones to exspensive computers,there are many diffrent ways to game. Some people prefer one over the other, whereas some people have the abilty to act like adults and can respect that we are all gamers. However, for those who can't act like that, here are the pros and cons of different ways to game

Consoles

First off, we have consoles: the two main ones being PS4 and XBOX, which is made by Sony and Microsoft respectively. There's also the weird cousin Nintendo, not that it is a bad thing (they just are). The good thing about consoles is that they are easy to use (for the most part): you just plug and play. This is not a bad thing however they do cap you between 30fps and 60fps (frames per secound). Although 60fps is good, this to me is a downside. Gaming on a PC you can choose your limit as long as your computer can handle it. Another huge, and I mean HUGE, downside is that they charge you anywhere from $60 to $120 to play online. This is crazy since you've already paid for the console and for the game, and now, you have to pay to play it.

Computer

Next off, we have computers (my personal favorite). Although computer cost more at first, they do end up saving you money in the long run. On pc games, the cost, on average, is 10% less on games. Also your fps are not limited, unlike on console as stated above. Computers also have more custimazation options with rgb(red,green and blue) which adds some really cool colors.

Virtual Reality

Last off we have VR (virtual reality), which is kind of a sub-catogory of the two since you can use a VR headset on both console and PC, I have both and I prefer my PC Vr headset over my Playstation VR since there is more games on the PC market for now. Playstation VR is also trash becuase you have to stay in one spot and can only look in about 270 degrees whereas VR on pc, it is 360 degrees (as long as you remember to step over the wire).
